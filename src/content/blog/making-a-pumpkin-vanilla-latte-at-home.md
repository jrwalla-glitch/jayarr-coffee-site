When fall rolls around, pumpkin-flavored everything takes over coffee shop menus -- and for good reason. But you don't need to stand in line or pay a premium for that cozy, seasonal flavor. Making a **pumpkin vanilla latte** at home is easier than you think, and you can customize it exactly to your taste.

## What You'll Need

The beauty of a homemade pumpkin vanilla latte is that it requires just a few simple ingredients:

- **Strong brewed coffee or espresso** -- the bolder the base, the better it stands up to the milk and flavoring
- **Milk** -- any variety works, though whole milk gives the richest, creamiest result
- **Pumpkin puree** -- real pumpkin puree, not pumpkin pie filling
- **Vanilla extract**
- **Sweetener of your choice** -- sugar, honey, or maple syrup all work well
- **Pumpkin pie spice** -- a blend of cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and cloves

## Putting It Together

Start by warming your milk on the stove or in the microwave. Whisk in a tablespoon or two of pumpkin puree, a splash of vanilla extract, your sweetener, and a pinch of pumpkin pie spice until everything is well combined and heated through. Froth the milk mixture if you have a frother -- it adds a nice cafe-style texture.

Brew your espresso or strong coffee, pour it into your favorite mug, and top it with the spiced pumpkin milk. A dusting of pumpkin pie spice or cinnamon on top finishes it off.

## Why Homemade Is Better

The advantage of making this at home is control. You decide how sweet it is, how much pumpkin flavor comes through, and whether you want real ingredients instead of artificial syrups. The result is a drink that's richer, more balanced, and costs a fraction of the coffee shop version.
