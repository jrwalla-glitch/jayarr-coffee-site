Colombia is one of the leading producers of high-end Arabica coffee in the world, accounting for an estimated **12% of all coffee** in the global industry. Despite sharing the Arabica label, Colombian coffees offer a surprising range of flavor profiles depending on the variety and growing region.

## The Varieties

The Arabica varieties cultivated in Colombia include **Typica**, **Caturra**, **Bourbon**, **Castillo**, and **Colombian Cultivar**. These coffees are often named after their specific farming regions, giving you a reliable way to predict what you will taste.

## What Colombian Coffee Tastes Like

While it is difficult to pin down a single definitive flavor profile, Colombian coffee has several recurring characteristics:

- **Smooth, mild, and vibrant** -- an easy-drinking cup
- **Low acidity** with a **medium, balanced body**
- **Fruity or citrusy aromas** with hints of spice
- **High flavor clarity** and a **pleasant sweetness**, thanks to the washed processing method used at most Colombian processing stations

## How Roast Level Changes the Cup

The beauty of Colombian coffee is its **versatility across roast levels**:

- **Medium to dark roasts** bring out a rich aroma with intense flavors. The acidity is muted by sweeter cocoa flavors and bright fruitiness.
- **Light roasts** highlight citrus fruit undertones and bright acidity, with the core bean flavor maintained alongside hints of caramel and cocoa.

Whether you prefer your coffee bold and rich or bright and fruity, Colombian beans have the range to deliver.
