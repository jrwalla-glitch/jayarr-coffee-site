## The Base

All three recipes start here:
- 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
- 1¾ cups whole milk (or half & half for extra richness)
- 1 tsp vanilla extract

Makes about 3 cups. Lasts 2-3 weeks refrigerated.

## 1. French Vanilla (The Go-To)

Base + 2 tsp vanilla extract + ¼ tsp almond extract (optional but recommended)

Pour everything into a mason jar, shake 30-45 seconds. Done.

The almond extract adds subtle depth that keeps it from tasting one-note. It's what separates "okay" from "genuinely delicious." Use pure vanilla extract, not imitation — the difference is noticeable in something this simple.

## 2. Salted Caramel (The Favorite)

Base + ¼ cup caramel sauce + ⅛ tsp sea salt

This one needs a blender — caramel sauce doesn't dissolve easily into cold milk. Blend condensed milk, caramel, vanilla, and salt on low for 15-20 seconds, add milk, blend 10 more seconds. No blender? Warm the milk slightly first and use a whisk.

The salt is non-negotiable. It's what makes caramel sing — cuts through sweetness, adds complexity. Use sea salt, not table salt. If you enjoy flavored lattes, this pairs beautifully with the technique in our [caramel brulee latte recipe](/blog/delicious-and-easy-caramel-brulee-latte/).

## 3. Hazelnut (The Crowd-Pleaser)

Base + 2 tsp hazelnut extract + ½ tsp vanilla + ⅛ tsp salt

Shake in a mason jar like the vanilla recipe. Spend a little extra on quality <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=hazelnut+extract+baking&tag=jayarrcoffee-20" rel="sponsored noopener">hazelnut extract</a> — cheap versions taste chemical and artificial.

## Dairy-Free Options

Replace whole milk with oat milk or full-fat coconut milk (best body). Use coconut-based or oat-based <a href="https://www.amazon.com/s?k=coconut+sweetened+condensed+milk&tag=jayarrcoffee-20" rel="sponsored noopener">sweetened condensed milk</a> products. Dairy-free versions separate more — shake well before each use.

## Storage

- 2-3 weeks refrigerated in a sealed jar
- Separation is normal — shake before each use
- Freeze for 2-3 months (blend after thawing to recombine)
- If it smells off or looks clumpy, toss it
