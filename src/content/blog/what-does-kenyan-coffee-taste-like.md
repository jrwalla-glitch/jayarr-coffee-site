## The Flavor Profile

Kenyan coffee is known for being **full-bodied, pleasantly acidic, and richly flavored**, with distinct floral notes and a flavor profile that ranges from sweet acidity to berry-toned finishes. Many coffee enthusiasts refer to Kenyan origin coffee as **"the connoisseur's cup"** — and the reputation is well earned. The combination of luxurious texture and distinct, punchy flavor makes it a standout among single origins.

## Kenya's Unique Grading System

What sets Kenya further apart is how seriously it takes quality control. The **Coffee Board of Kenya** invented a class system for grading beans before they reach the international market. After harvest, all coffee is sorted by size, shape, color, and density using electronic or mechanical evaluation. Premium beans are identified and separated, while defective or low-quality beans are discarded.

The larger coffee beans are graded separately, based on the principle that **bigger beans contain more aromatic oils**, which bolster both flavor profile and aroma. The highest grade — **Kenyan AA** — represents both the best quality and the largest beans within the Kenyan system.

Kenya remains one of the few African countries growing and exporting high-quality coffee in large volumes, offering some of the most spectacular flavor profiles available anywhere in the world.
