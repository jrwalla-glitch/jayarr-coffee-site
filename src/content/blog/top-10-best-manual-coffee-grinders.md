There's an old saying in coffee circles: the grinder is more important than the coffee maker. A weak grinder can limit even the most skilled barista, while a good one lays the foundation for everything that follows -- hazelnut, caramel, chocolate, fruity, floral, smoky, whatever flavors you're chasing. We put ten of the most popular manual hand grinders through their paces and ranked them on particle uniformity, ease of use, and overall design.

## How We Tested

For the purposes of our review, we used a **coarse grind** throughout all tests. The reasoning is straightforward: finer grounds tend to look uniform regardless of grinder quality, while coarseness exposes any real flaws in the burrs. We evaluated each grinder individually before picking our overall favorite.

## 1. Porlex Mini Stainless Steel Coffee Grinder

The **[Porlex Mini](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Porlex+Mini+Stainless+Steel+Coffee+Grinder&tag=jayarrcoffee-20)** is a small but formidable piece of Japanese grinding machinery. Its stainless steel body is anti-static, which saves you the annoying task of scraping and tapping to remove grounds that stick to the container. A heavy-duty ceramic conical burr does the work.

At just over **200 grams (7.9 ounces)**, this is arguably the most portable grinder on the list -- we were actually able to fit it inside an AeroPress. It features **13 grind settings** adjusted by turning a dial and excels at most settings, making it a solid choice for pour over, AeroPress, or French press. Grinding is easy on the hands, though finer grinds may require some effort.

## 2. Orphan Espresso Lido 3

The **[Orphan Espresso Lido 3](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Orphan+Espresso+Lido+3&tag=jayarrcoffee-20)** is a towering piece of equipment. It's not so large that it needs its own travel bag, but it does take up a chunk of valuable space. The plastic body houses a large burr regulated by a **stepless grind adjuster** -- you unlock the locking ring, adjust, then lock it again. The lock-unlock mechanism isn't the most elegant, but it works.

The Lido 3 is a **particularly fast grinder** that easily chews through beans. The bean hopper is well designed and keeps beans where they belong. However, at **$185**, the build doesn't quite feel as premium as some alternatives at similar price points. It does the job, but the size and grind adjustment hold it back.

## 3. Timemore Chestnut G1

The **[Timemore Chestnut G1](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Timemore+Chestnut+G1+Coffee+Grinder&tag=jayarrcoffee-20)** is simply gorgeous. The walnut bottom blends seamlessly with the aluminum top -- it actually won the **Red Dot Design Award in 2017**. But does the beauty have the brains to match?

Absolutely. Cranking is seamless, the grind is fluid, and the twin bearings with precision-milled steel burrs produced **surprisingly impressive particle uniformity**. At around **$159**, the Chestnut ranks on the higher side, but the experience is rewarding and second to none. One note: cleaning coffee oils off the wooden container isn't the most pleasant task.

## 4. Hario Mini Mill Slim

Easy to handle, lightweight, compact, and sturdy -- the **[Hario Mini Mill Slim](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Hario+Mini+Mill+Slim+Coffee+Grinder&tag=jayarrcoffee-20)** impresses right out of the box. Like the Porlex Mini, it uses ceramic burrs for durability. The grind consistency was relatively even and accurate.

The downside? The grinder **lacks markings for grind size**, a poor oversight for such a simple feature. You'll need to run some trial and error to discover your ideal settings. That said, at **under $40** with a two-cup capacity, it offers immense value for your morning ritual.

## 5. Zassenhaus Santiago Manual Coffee Mill

Timeless and vintage, the **[Zassenhaus Santiago](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Zassenhaus+Santiago+Manual+Coffee+Mill&tag=jayarrcoffee-20)** boasts a traditional mahogany body with a carbon steel finish for modern flair. At **$130**, it has a 40-gram capacity (roughly two to three cups) and delivers consistent grinds across a huge number of settings -- whether espresso or Turkish, this grinder handles it.

A drawer at the front captures the grounds for easy transfer to your coffee maker. At **952 grams**, though, this is firmly a countertop grinder -- not something you'd carry around in your bag. The striking design, quality finish, and **25-year warranty** are testament to its build quality. If you have a soft spot for antique or vintage aesthetics, the Santiago will fit perfectly into your kitchen decor.

## 6. Handground Precision Coffee Grinder

The **[Handground Precision](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Handground+Precision+Coffee+Grinder&tag=jayarrcoffee-20)** offers some of the best bang for your buck on this list. The transparent body is appealing -- it's genuinely fun to watch the ceramic burrs chew through beans. With **15 grind settings**, it produces evenly ground coffee suitable for espresso, AeroPress, French press, and pour over.

It's on the larger side for counter space, but unlike most manual grinders, **the handle rotates vertically**. This is actually more comfortable and allowed us to grind at a solid pace while maintaining stability.

## 7. Hario Skerton Ceramic Coffee Mill

**Hario** has a strong reputation in the coffee industry, and the [Skerton Ceramic Coffee Mill](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=Hario+Skerton+Ceramic+Coffee+Mill&tag=jayarrcoffee-20) lives up to it. The manual grinder is easy to use and compact. Grounds were relatively consistent for espresso, though it lagged behind on coarser settings.

Like the Mini Mill Slim, the Skerton **does not have a coarseness indicator** -- it's purely trial and error. Be prepared for a learning curve.

## 8. JavaPresse Manual Coffee Grinder

The **[JavaPresse](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=JavaPresse+Manual+Coffee+Grinder&tag=jayarrcoffee-20)** is a fan favorite with tens of thousands of positive reviews on Amazon. It offers a **40-gram hopper capacity**, ceramic conical burr, and **18 grind settings**. The hand crank detaches from the stainless steel body, making it ideal for on-the-go grinding.

We found the click system for grind settings a bit tricky to work out, which may take some getting used to. But if you're looking for an affordable, simple, functional, fuss-free grinder that gets the job done, JavaPresse checks all the boxes.

## 9. ROK Coffee Grinder

Calling all manual brewing enthusiasts: the **[ROK Coffee Grinder](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=ROK+Coffee+Grinder&tag=jayarrcoffee-20)** is designed for a seamless grinding experience. From the ergonomically designed vertical handle to the non-slip ring for stability and indefinite hopper capacity, it has everything you need to grind with ease and speed. It also features **48mm stainless steel conical burrs**.

The downsides: portability is limited, and the grind was **relatively inconsistent at times**. At **$250**, it's a significant investment.

## 10. 1Zpresso Q2 Manual Coffee Grinder

The **[1Zpresso Q2](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=1Zpresso+Q2+Manual+Coffee+Grinder&tag=jayarrcoffee-20)** is a premium grinder built for backpackers, campers, and anyone who needs their coffee on the go. The build quality is unquestionable, featuring a **38mm stainless steel burr**. As a tiny, travel-friendly grinder, it has a 20-gram capacity -- great for small batches but not ideal for groups. At **$99**, we'd call it a steal.

## Our Verdict

If we had to pick just one out of all ten manual coffee grinders, we'd go with the **Porlex Mini**. It's good value for the money, portable, functional, and durable. Sure, there are a few areas where it lagged behind others on the list -- but if we could only take one device home, the Porlex would be the one.
