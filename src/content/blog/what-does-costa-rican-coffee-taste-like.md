Costa Rica is widely regarded as one of the happiest countries in the world, and we like to think their amazing coffee plays a role in that. If any coffee connoisseur were pressed to rank single origin coffees by quality, Costa Rican coffee would comfortably hold its own against any other origin in the world.

## A Commitment to Quality

Here is a fact that says everything about Costa Rica's approach to coffee: production of non-100% Arabica coffee is **illegal** under the country's laws. That is not a recommendation -- it is legislation. Every bean that comes out of Costa Rica meets the Arabica standard.

Costa Rica was one of the earliest coffee-growing countries in Central America, with the first coffee beans dating as far back as **1779**. The industry thrived thanks to near-perfect climatic conditions, and in less than half a century, coffee overtook cacao, tobacco, and sugar as the country's largest and most valuable export. Even Starbucks maintains an extensive network of plantations there.

## The Flavor Profile

Costa Rican coffee offers a diverse range of flavors depending on the processing method and growing region. In general, you can expect **floral or sweet notes**, a **lighter medium body**, and a **mild acidity**. Simply put, if you sample Costa Rican coffee, you are in for a treat.

## How Roast Level Affects the Cup

One of the things that makes Costa Rican coffee stand out is its **versatility across roast levels**. Whether you go dark, medium, or light, you are going to get a great cup.

- **Medium Roast** highlights the body and complex flavors with a pleasant sweetness that brings everything together.
- **Dark Roast** washed beans deliver a smooth, deep cup that brews beautifully with an espresso machine or French press.
- **Light Roast** beans showcase a mild acidity and a clean, sweet taste with fruity notes.

## A Rich History in Specialty Coffee

Costa Rican coffee is far from a newcomer to the specialty scene. The country has been producing premium, eclectic coffee beans for decades, supported by government programs and ideal growing conditions. It is a heritage crop with a long track record of excellence -- and one of the most reliable single origins you can buy.
