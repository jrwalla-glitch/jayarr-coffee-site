Rwanda is a small, scenic country in East Africa that overcame one of the most devastating chapters in modern history to become one of the continent's fastest-growing economies. What many people don't realize is that coffee played a crucial role in that rebuilding effort -- and today, Rwandan beans have earned a well-deserved place among the world's most sought-after specialty coffees.

## A Difficult History

Missionaries introduced coffee to Rwanda in the early 1900s, but unlike countries such as Ethiopia or Kenya, the Rwandan coffee industry was far from a success story in its early decades. Growth stagnated, and colonialists attempted to exploit the industry by producing **low-grade coffee in high volumes** with little regard for quality or the farmers who grew it.

## The Turning Point

In the early 2000s, the Rwandan government initiated programs to encourage coffee farmers to shift their focus from quantity to quality -- from bulk commodity production to **specialty coffees**. The results have been remarkable. The favorable climate, high altitude, and high-quality wet processing methods now contribute to a truly superb cup.

## Flavor Profile

**Rwandan coffee is full-bodied with a heavy, syrupy mouthfeel**, a character associated with the Bourbon coffee variety grown throughout the country. On the flavor front, expect bright notes of **lime** accompanied by fruity or floral flavors. Don't be surprised to find hints of white chocolate, nuttiness, mandarin orange, cinnamon, clove, dates, or cantaloupe. The profile is rounded out by a **caramel aftertaste**, and the acidity tends toward the higher side with a clean, crisp feel.

## Why It Matters

Rwanda's coffee story is about more than just great beans. From the country's challenging history to the government's commitment to quality, from optimal growing conditions to meticulous wet processing, every cup carries a heartfelt story of resilience and renewal. These beans deserve a spot in the specialty coffee conversation -- and in your kitchen cabinet.
